# Urban Mobility and Economic Productivity Analysis

## Project Overview

This project analyzes the relationship between urban mobility indicators and economic performance across major Latin American cities.

The objective was to explore whether traffic congestion, travel delays, unemployment rates, air quality, population size, and GDP per capita could provide insights into economic productivity and urban development.

The project demonstrates a complete analytics workflow including data cleaning, transformation, integration, exploratory analysis, visualization, and business interpretation.

---

## Business Question

Can urban mobility indicators provide meaningful insights into economic productivity and quality of life across Latin American cities?

## Dataset Description

The project combines two primary datasets:

### OECD City Economy Data

Contains economic and demographic indicators for major Latin American cities, including:

* GDP per capita
* Unemployment rates
* Population statistics
* PM2.5 air quality indicators

### TomTom Traffic Data

Contains urban mobility and traffic metrics, including:

* Traffic congestion levels
* Travel delay indicators
* Traffic intensity measurements
* Travel times

Both datasets were cleaned, standardized, and merged to create a unified analytical dataset.

## Key Findings

### Significant Differences in Urban Mobility

The analysis identified substantial variation in congestion and travel delays among Latin American cities.

### Economic Productivity Does Not Guarantee Lower Congestion

No strong relationship was observed between GDP per capita and traffic congestion levels. Several economically productive cities continue to experience significant mobility challenges.

### Mobility Is Influenced by Multiple Factors

The results suggest that population density, transportation infrastructure, urban planning, and commuting patterns may have a stronger influence on congestion than economic output alone.
